About Me

In the midst of my journey, I found myself torn between the worlds of software engineering and digital media. This story encapsulates my exploration, a path shaped by passion, curiosity, and a quest for self-discovery.

During my internship at Snap One, I delved into the structured world of software engineering, solving problems and relishing victories. Yet, a subtle yearning for something more lingered. The turning point came as the Art Recommenders Leader at AI@UCF, where technology met creativity. Being able to use my previous knowledge of art to craft a multimedia recommender AI became more than an engineering feat; it became a canvas for artistic expression.

The struggle to choose between these two worlds became my personal challenge. Software engineering offered precision and structured elegance, while digital media beckoned with design thinking and graphic creation.

Amidst this uncertainty, I pursued a Digital Media minor, exploring the creative unknown. Pixels on the screen became my playground, and I discovered that design principles could be as captivating as lines of code.

As I stand at this crossroads, I invite you into my journey, where each decision is a brushstroke in the portrait of my uncertainty. Choosing between software engineering and digital media isn't just about career paths; it's about shaping an identity that resonates with precision and creativity.
